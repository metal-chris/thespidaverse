## VERSION 1: "The Comfort Zone" (Emphasis on Personal Connection)

### Copy:

Growing up, my dad and I bonded over nerdy stuff. He had his baseball cards and Magic: The Gathering collection, I had my Pokémon and Yu-Gi-Oh! decks. We'd spend hours talking about the things we were passionate about—comparing pulls, debating strategies, geeking out over whatever we were into that week.

That's what pop culture has always been for me: a way to connect. A language we speak when regular words don't cut it.

When life got heavy—and ***it did***—I found myself often turning to Spider-Man comics. Not because they had all the answers, but because Peter Parker never gave up. No matter how bad things got, no matter how much the world pushed back, he kept swinging. That resilience became something I needed to see, over and over again.

**The Spidaverse** is my way of paying that forward. It's where I share the movies, games, anime, manga, and music that got me through—and continue to get me through. Every piece of media gets a **Web Rating** out of 100, visualized as a spider web that fills based on the score. Not because I want to debate whether something deserves an 87 or a 91, but because sometimes you just need to quantify how much something mattered to you. The emphasis being that these things connect us to one another.

I'm not here to prove anything right or wrong. I'm not here to win arguments in the comments (the internet's already got enough of that). I'm just here to nerd out with you about the stuff we love. To share why a "bad" movie hit different for me. To put you on to that J-Pop/Rap artist you've been sleeping on. To defend *Batman v Superman* against all odds (yes, really).

Spider-Man inspires hope. I'm not him—I'm just trying to be someone he'd respect while being my own person. Because anyone can wear the mask. Anyone can be Spider-Man. And we're all in this together, connected by the stories that shape us.

Welcome to the web!

## VERSION 2: "The Analyst's Notebook" (Emphasis on Thoughtful Sharing)

### Copy:

My dad collected baseball cards and Magic: The Gathering. I collected Pokémon and Yu-Gi-Oh!. We'd compare our pulls, talk strategy, share what we were passionate about. That's how I learned that the best conversations aren't about winning arguments—they're about sharing what matters to you and seeing what resonates.

**The Spidaverse** is built on that principle.

This isn't a debate platform. It's not a place where I'm trying to convince you that my take is objectively correct or that your favorite thing is actually mid. The internet already has enough of that energy. This is a space where I share my honest thoughts on the movies I watch, the games I play, the anime I binge, the manga I read, and the music I vibe to—and you can take from it what resonates.

Every piece of media gets a **Web Rating** out of 100, visualized as a spider web that fills based on the score. The number matters, but the *why* behind it matters more. Why does a "perfect" movie sometimes leave you cold? Why does a 6/10 game become your comfort food? Why does a mediocre album suddenly hit different three years later?

I'm here to explore those questions, not answer them definitively.

When I was going through some heavy stuff, I found myself turning to Spider-Man comics. Not because they had solutions, but because Peter Parker's resilience—his refusal to give up even when everything was falling apart—gave me something to hold onto. That's what good media does: it meets you where you are and gives you what you need, even if you didn't know you needed it.

Spider-Man inspires hope. I'm not trying to be him, but I aspire to be someone he'd respect—someone who shows up, does the work, and stays true to themselves. Because anyone can wear the mask. Anyone can be Spider-Man. We're all connected by the stories that shape us.

Some things you should know about me:
- I think J-Pop/Rap is criminally underrated
- I genuinely believe *Batman v Superman* was a good movie that got undeserved hate
- I will defend not putting pineapple on pizza against all odds
- I'm more interested in sharing my perspective than debating it

The site runs on **Miles Mode** (light) and **Venom Mode** (dark). Toggle the spider emblem in the header to switch themes. Built with Next.js, Sanity, and way too much custom CSS—but that's half the fun.

Thanks for being here. Let's nerd out together.
